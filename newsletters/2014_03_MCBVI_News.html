    FROM THE PRESIDENT

    By Joe Sibley

    I am writing this with a lot of snow out my front door. Hopefully some of this will be gone by the time you read this. Oh well, it's winter in Michigan. I like the fact that we have four distinct seasons, but I'm ready to move on to the next season. It's OK though I am blessed to have a wonderful wife and many awesome friends in MCBVI.

    Speaking of seasons before you know it our state convention will be coming up. The convention will be September 25 - 28 at the Four Points by Sheraton in Kalamazoo. Much more will be coming out soon; the convention committee is working hard to plan a great weekend for you. We work hard to keep the costs down so I hope to see you at convention this year.

    As you know in the past few years we have held an "event" on Thursday night to open our convention. This year I am excited to announce that we plan to offer "Out of Sight Night" on Thursday September 25. There will be a dinner and entertainment by a great band. This will be a fun activity for our convention attendees as well as an outreach to the community and will raise some much needed funds too.

    The band is Beyond Sight, led by Steve and Rhonda (Selby) Scott who are both blind. This band is exploding in popularity and we will have a great time that night. Watch for details. Don't forget that Kim Charlson, president of ACB, will be joining us for our convention.

    I am a little sad looking at this convention as it will be my last as your president. At the end of this convention I will hand the proverbial gavel over to the new president that you will elect. I will always be grateful for the opportunity that you have given me in my 3 terms as MCBVI president, and I look forward to the next chapter in this organization's history. MCBVI changes lives, it changed mine, let's all work together to take it to the next level. Tell your friends that aren't members, let's bring in more members to the family, the more we have the more we can accomplish to the benefit of all whom are blind or visually impaired in Michigan.

    This issue is being put together by Malaina VanderWal, our assistant editor. Malaina and Donna Rose are a great team, I am proud of the MCBVI News. I want to thank both of them for their great service! Smile today, it never hurts! Joe

    MEMORIAL TO JEANETTE GENO

    By Gerald Clay

    As our path with her life on earth ends, and Jeanette's new path begins, if I ever had a choice in a friend it would be Jeanette. Her strong belief in the church and God will help her in finding her way to heaven knowing she will be met at the gates with a smile.

    Jeanette will be remembered for her kindness and outgoing attitude. Her strength in life will carry on in heaven and she will be remembered for her acts of kindness. On earth she will be remembered as a warm and friendly leader in our community.

    Jeanette was more than a member in the Blue Water League of the Blind where she led this organization for many years where she will be tremendously missed. Jeanette's husband's love and devotion is an example of Jeanette's love of life, a precedent that should be followed by all.

    Her battle in life and her ability to overcome and face life to the end is a role model to all who knew her. Jeanette's laugh, and "hi guys" will be remembered by all. In our hearts Jeanette is not gone for she has left especial part of her in everybody that new her and loved her. Jeanette will be greatly missed throughout the MCBVI and the Blue Water League of the Blind community. Go forth and rejoice with God in heaven our friend.

    THE EASY, ALMOST NO EFFORT FUNDRAISER

    By: Dr. Ronald E. Milliman, MCBVI member

    This "Easy, Almost No Effort Fundraiser" is a way you can help fund your ACB state affiliate without having to do much of anything. You don't have to ask people for money. You don't have to participate in any fundraising walks, or sell raffle tickets or yard sales, or anything of the kind. It requires just a tiny bit of information when initially enrolling in the program and after that, it is on automatic! You don't have to do anything more!

    This is one fundraiser that if it sounds too good to be true, really is true! In fact, this fundraiser is so good that you can not only help generate much needed revenue for your MCBVI state affiliate, but you can even help your affiliate's parent organization, the National ACB, all at the same time!

    When participating in the ACB's Monthly Monetary Support (MMS) Program, you simply enroll in the program and an amount, designated by you and completely controlled by you, is automatically deducted from your bank account or charged to your credit or debit card each month. It starts when you want it to begin and will end upon your request. It is so easy. Once you enroll in the program, nothing more you or your MCBVI affiliate need to do. Everything is completely handled by our financial office. If you ever want to change the amount of your contribution, you can by simply contacting our ACB financial office. You can have up to 50% of the net amount of your contribution designated to support your state affiliate.

    PLUS: While they last!!

    As an expression of our appreciation for your participation in the ACB MMS Program, your affiliate president, your ACB National President, your ACB national Board, and the ACB's MMS Program Committee, are delighted to send you a pocket-sized, FM Scanner Radio when we receive your first contribution. This hi-quality, little FM radio comes complete with Braille and print operating instructions, Ear Bud earphones and batteries for your FM listening enjoyment.

    That's Not All…

    Anyone who has met the following criteria between the end of the 2013 ACB national conference and convention and the close of this next year's convention will be placed in the grand prize drawing. In the most recent years, the winners of these drawings have won such gifts as a Hamilton Beach talking microwave, a Humanware Victor Reader Stream, a GW Micro BookSense, a Touch Memo, a Bookport Plus, and an iPad. All you need to do is either enroll in the MMS Program with a minimum contribution of $10 per month, or if you are already enrolled in the MMS Program, just increase your existing contribution by a minimum of $5 per month.

    How Your Contributions are Used to Benefit You…

    By supporting your state's affiliate, you are also benefiting yourself. Your state affiliate needs funding to support its many activities, such as on-going public education campaigns, scholarships, social events, defraying the costs associated with conducting your MCBVI state affiliate's convention, producing your state affiliate's newsletter and other promotional and educational materials.

    The funds you contribute are also used to support the many benefits you get from being a member of the American Council of the Blind (ACB). These benefits to you include:

    • The ACB Braille Forum, our Flagship publication
    • Support for ACB Radio, our exclusive internet radio network
    • Support your annual, national conference & convention
    • Support for the paid staff and offices
    • Support for legislation that benefit you and all blind and low-vision people
    • Support to fight to maintain and increase your SSI and SSDI benefits
    • Support to make accessible paper currency a reality
    • Support for the fight for accessible point of service devices, e.g. ATM machines
    • Support for audible traffic signals
    • Support the fight to increase the use of descriptive audio for TV and movies

    When participating in this "Easy, Almost No Effort Fundraiser. You can support your state affiliate and the national organization, both at the same time, with a single contribution, two-for-one! Some members can afford more or less than others, and we certainly realize and respect that. All we are asking is that you help fund your state affiliate and our national programs with whatever monthly amount you can afford. We equally value everyone's contribution. To give you some idea, currently, contributions range from $10 per month and go all the way up to over $100 per month.     FDA APPROVES TREATMENT FOR NON-24 CIRCADIAN RHYTHYM DISORDER

    Non-24 is highly prevalent among the totally blind

    WASHINGTON, January 31, 2014 - Vanda Pharmaceuticals Inc. (VANDA) (NASDAQ: VNDA) today announced that the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has approved HETLIOZ™ (tasimelteon) 20mg capsules for the treatment of Non-24-Hour Sleep-Wake Disorder (Non-24). HETLIOZ™ is the first FDA approved medication for Non-24.

    Non-24 was first described more than 60 years ago, and is a chronic, circadian rhythm disorder resulting from the misalignment of the endogenous master body clock to the 24-hour day, disrupting the sleep-wake cycle. Non-24 affects the majority of totally blind individuals and it is estimated that approximately 80,000 Americans have the disorder.

    Thinking Of Getting an IPhone or IPad

    By Malaina VanderWal

    Are you thinking of purchasing an IPhone or IPad? Perhaps you have just purchased one but have not unlocked all the helpful apps accessible to you? In this article I am going to discuss an app I have found very useful for some of those tasks where it was helpful to have a sighted person but no one was around. This app is called FaceTime and it is installed on the IPhone or IPad out of the box.

    FaceTime is an app allowing two apple devices to make video calls. It is similar to Skype in the respect it is a call made over a wifi or a cell carrier network. It requires a phone number of the person with an IPhone or an email address for IPad users.

    For instance, if you want to FaceTime with your IPhone and a family member with an IPhone or IPad you do the following. One double tap the FaceTime app on your home screen or location of the app. Two, double tap the contacts tab found at the bottom of your screen. This is the third tab from the left or the first tab from the right. It has three tabs along the bottom of the screen favorites, recent and contacts. Once the contacts tab is located double tap the name of the person you wish to call. The person's IPhone number or email address associated with their apple device must be in your contacts. Three once you double tap the name you want to call you're presented with the contact info. Four swipe from left to right with one finger until you find the facetime button. Five, double tap the facetime button and wait for the person to answer. A little story of how facetime has helped me in life.

    A few months ago I went to the house where two of my friends live. The three of us were going to make some tater tots in the oven. The dial however was not completely marked and we needed a different temperature from what was already labeled. I decided to use facetime to help us. I called my dad on his IPad and with his help we set the oven dial close to where it needed to be set. I held my phone so my dad could see the numbers on the dial and with some patients on his part and laughing on mine we set the dial! I have also used facetime to help me with identifying money when my IBill money identifier was not working. During our state convention last year I used facetime to talk to my nieces! Although I could not see them they could see me and it was great to talk with them for a few!

    The sound quality is usually very good and clearer than a regular phone call. Try it you might be surprised how useful this pre-installed app can benefit your life! Other apps will follow in the next issue of the newsletter. If you have something you would enjoy learning about on the IPhone contact me at Malaina@iserv.net .

    PUBLICATIONS & PUBLIC RELATIONS

    By Donna Rose, Chairperson

    There is no public relations information to report to you at this time. But, there is news to report on the Publications front involving this newsletter. At the MCBVI Winter Board Meeting in January the Board decided, with my and Malaina's recommendation, to put out three, instead of four, newsletters this year and re-evaluate this decision in early 2015, with the Board to determine if this change will be more successful for our budget and for timely publishing.

    Also at that meeting we discussed the types of issues which hold up the newsletter writing and production process. Most of us involved have contributed to the delays in one way or another. As I have said on many occasions, I love working on and being editor of the newsletter. However, if we truly want a newsletter, and many members have acknowledged they do, we need to take the deadlines for submissions seriously. Although we are all volunteers sharing our time and talents, cooperation is of the utmost importance in order to complete any of our organization's business. This includes the newsletter too!

    What does it take to write and produce our newsletter? First, Malaina and I gather info for a few months from a variety of sources. We send out an email reminder about the approaching deadline to those who have email addresses registered with us and ask people to remind those who may not have email. Near the deadline time we discuss our plans for each issue. After she gathers the committee reports and chapter updates she emails them to me along with other articles she has written or gathered herself. Sometimes you might get a call or email from her as she tries to track everyone down for the necessary information about committees and chapters.

    We try to include as much information as possible in each newsletter. When making choices about which types of articles are going to be included, we ask ourselves, is this info pertinent to our members here in Michigan or related to our organization, is it information that might be helpful to those who may not have easy access to it, and is it interesting enough? When looking ahead at each deadline I try and leave blocks of time in my calendar just after the deadline so that all of these items can be edited, assembled, and formatted. When items roll in late it effects the time I have set aside. Then I must fit it into my schedule as best I can, which can also delay the entire process.

    When I finish the final draft I send it to Assistant Editor Malaina and President Joe for their take on it so we can change, omit and/or add things one last time before sending it for production.

    Once it has been put into final form it is given to President Joe and First Lady Mary for brailing and copying it into large print. Then to Asst. Treasurer Wayne who oversees the recording and copying of the CD version.

    Charis makes the mailing labels for the different formats and sends out the email version. Joe mails out the braille and large print formats. Wayne sends out his CD copies.

    It is truly an assembly line of jobs, and at the end your newsletter arrives at your door. So you can see how one glitch in this process can have an adverse effect on production timeliness.

    It is for this reason we need to make the deadline the deadline. Items for the next newsletter which are not submitted by or on the deadline day will be held for the following newsletter. We will remind you in advance, Put the next deadline in each newsletter and try to track you down as it gets close. But ultimately, this is your newsletter and it will be your responsibility to get stuff to us by the deadline.

    Malaina and I will need to talk to the board about a couple of things which could help the entire production process. Since our organization is running in a deficit every year, we think these are two very good ideas. First, at a state convention a few years back the membership requested they wanted better communication from the board. They wanted a summary of each Board meeting's minutes included with each newsletter. We also include all of our convention resolutions in the newsletter following the state convention as well. The minutes take up about 10 or more braille pages and sometimes the resolutions even more. We would like to hear from the board at the April meeting about placing the approved minutes from each Board meeting and resolutions from our conventions on the internet, and only offer them in alternative formats upon request. About half of our members have internet access, and we feel then we will only be sending them out to those who truly want to read them. It will save us money and time to if we adopt this new policy. Think about it! We will be discussing this at April's Board meeting which will be by telephone. You are invited to attend. If you don't have email please contact an Executive Board member about the phone number and passcode to enter the conference. We thank you in advance for considering the concerns raised in this report. The next newsletter deadline will be June 15, 2014.

    Summary of the MCBVI Winter Board Meeting

    Submitted by Secretary, Debbie Robinson

    The Michigan Council of the Blind and Visually Impaired Winter Board Meeting was conducted by teleconference on January 18, 2014. It began at 10:06 a.m. and ended at 1:25 p.m. Four of the five chapters were present, and two officers were absent. We had about 20 participants. The meeting agenda was approved as read, and the summer board meeting minutes were approved as e-mailed.

    President Sibley reported we lost long-time member Jeanette Geno yesterday morning after battling cancer. We had a few seconds of silence for Michael Geno and family. Michael Smith shared funeral arrangements/location. He is planning on recording the service for those who cannot make it. A memorial page will be up on the Blue Water League web site with tributes. Several updates were given regarding MCBVI members who have either had surgery or health concerns.

    This is Joe's last year as president. He shared goals for 2014: To start at least one new chapter, to continue developing the new MCBVI brochure, to have One major fundraiser this year, and to launch an MCBVI student chapter. He discussed a possible Thursday night convention event/major fundraiser this year in Kalamazoo. This would be a concert with two acts: One featuring someone who is visually impaired and a headliner group. He is in contact with people at the State Theater and Miller Auditorium at Western Michigan University. He had no information on concert expenses. The concert would need sponsors such as a company sponsor and a media sponsor to market it. Later in the meeting, it was decided to give the executive committee authority to make the final decision about the concert. Another option is to have an event with entertainment at the hotel which would not be as expensive.

    We decided to do committee reports since Charis was not present yet. Casey reported on the MCBVI convention. Please see your December newsletter for a report on last year's convention. About 60 people registered. He asked for $381.94 from MCBVI to bring the convention account to $1,500.00. The 2014 and 2015 conventions will be in Kalamazoo at the Four Points by Sheraton. This year's dates are Thursday, September 25th through Sunday, September 28th. The hotel rooms will be $79 (this amount changed to $75 post winter board meeting) per night. We can bring in only peanuts, mints and pop and bottled water for the hospitality room. We will have to buy everything else from the hotel, but the cost goes toward the meeting room. The exhibits may only be on Friday because they have to be in the same room as the meetings. We may offer meal options because the restaurant is not open all day. Casey is looking for committee members. There was no activity for grants or scholarships. We still do not have a grants chair.

    Casey reported regarding legislation that the Civil Service Commission has taken more things away from State employees and retirees. There are healthcare issues with Obama Care. On the federal level, there is HR3749 regarding inclusion of devices for the blind and visually impaired like CCTVs under Medicare. This is a 5-year trial study program, with about $12 million made available. We need to support this, so contact your house representative in Congress to support this bill. He also discussed the Accessible prescription drug Act. We are in the middle of an 18-month best practices process. It is important for MCBVI members to contact pharmacies and Envision America to see which pharmacies will or will not be part of this process. It is important to determine what the barriers are. There are some barriers with insurance companies or the interpretation of federal law regarding controlled substances. Not all pharmacies will do mail orders of controlled substances. The only way to know is if people participate and share their experiences with Envision America. Best practices are not requirements that every pharmacy has to meet.

    Be sure to get your dues and other information to Charis by February 15th to be counted. She needs to know what format of Braille Forum and MCBVI news you want. She can accept credit card payments this year but will add a dollar to each transaction to cover credit card fees. We have around 164 members.

    After much discussion, it was decided to produce three newsletters in 2014, rather than four and to re-examine this in January of 2015. The deadlines will be February 15, June 15 and October 15. Work continues on the MCBVI brochure. The mission statement committee has not met yet. Writing a memorial tribute for Jeanette in the MCBVI newsletter and/or the Braille Forum was discussed.

    Wayne reported he is working on a proposed bylaw change to allow the assistant treasurer and assistant secretary voting members at board meetings, even when the secretary and treasurer are present. This will be presented at the summer board meeting in July, with the final vote at convention. John and Charis are working on other changes.

    Malaina reported the resolutions passed last fall were published in the newsletter. She has to determine who will serve on the committee. The Accessible prescriptions and accessible labels resolution did not get addressed in a timely manner.

    Karla reported no candidates are running at this point. The offices up for election are president, secretary and assistant treasurer.

    Jackie reported the goals for technology are to continue using a wireless microphone for workshops and break-out sessions as we did at our last convention; to broadcast more information to the radio audience, including streaming interviews with exhibitors; expanding use of MCBVI radio for programming other than convention material. Another goal was creating an MCBVI radio page on our web site with an accessible link to MCBVI radio.

    They are looking into the advantages of purchasing and using Station Playlist software while streaming. It is an automated program. A basic version runs around $100, and is very accessible for JAWS users.

    Technology is requesting a budget amount of $600.00 to upgrade things.

    Charis reported on fund development. She discussed ACB's MMS program. In her financial report, the Investment portfolio total balance on 12/31/13 was $39,515.18. Total income from 01/01/13 to 12/31/13 was $4,130.16. Total expenses from that same time period were $5,181.22, resulting in a net loss of $1,050.54. There was much discussion on toll free usage, with the amount being about $500.

    She reviewed the proposed budget for 2014. Total proposed income was $5,000.00. Total proposed expenses were $8,218.00, with a projected deficit of $3,218.54. One item was budgeted of an amount of $500 for attending the ACB midyear board meeting/legislative seminar. Originally John and Joe were thinking of going, having submitted a much larger amount. The executive committee decided to give Sara Conrad this $500.00 as a stipend. She will be there anyway as part of ACB's board. She agreed to represent MCBVI as well. There was much discussion on the budget/deficit. The proposed budget was approved as submitted, as was the above financial report.

    Casey reported on Opportunities Unlimited for the Blind (OUB) activities. We made a donation last year to cosponsor a music camp. Due to changes the State made, OUB will be using Indian Trails rather than Camp Tuhsmeheta this summer. They are thinking of setting up a schedule of five weeks of camp starting in June. It costs about $1,200 per person per week for camp. It was decided to table this till we get more information on camps and sponsorship levels.

    We need to know by April the delegate from each chapter. Chapter charters were discussed. There is interest in beginning a chapter in Traverse City and in the Jackson area.

    After much discussion it was decided to have the July board meeting by teleconference. It will be, due to the ACB convention, on 07/26/14. It was also decided to move the spring board meeting date to Saturday, 04/26/14 due to Easter. This will also be by teleconference.
